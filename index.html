ABOUT ME

Introduction

I am Felix Otieno from Nairobi, Kenya. I am a web developer by profession and an entrepreneur focused on problem-solving. My curiosity about how websites are created, managed, and designed inspired me to become a web developer, and I am passionate about this field. Debugging code in JavaScript encouraged me to pursue entrepreneurship through design thinking and problem-solving in IT. Besides watching football and traveling, coding has become one of my hobbies.

My Story

I began my education in 2004 when I was enrolled at Little Rock, where I studied until 2006. Afterward, in 2007, I joined Ayany Estate Primary School and continued there until 2014, earning a Certificate of Primary Education upon completion. In 2015, I joined Olympic High School after completing primary school. I studied there until 2018 and earned a Certificate of Secondary Education, marking the completion of my secondary school education. After completing secondary school, I engaged in a waste management program where I underwent training in proper waste handling and management. Additionally, I received training in financial literacy skills. My eagerness to learn and acquire new skills motivated me to enroll at Tunapanda Institute, where I aimed to gain more knowledge and sharpen my skills for a continuous learning career. At Tunapanda, I earned a Certificate of Completion in ICT, for which I was grateful. I continued my studies by enrolling at Franky Foundation to learn E-Waste Management, a field I found enjoyable and aligned with my career path. Learning at Tunapanda Institute introduced me to web development, programming, and software development, which helped me understand how computers work and their impact on society. I found these topics in computer science to be enjoyable and motivating, prompting me to delve deeper into web development and programming languages such as JavaScript and Python. Through my studies in web development, I learned how to create appealing websites using HTML for structure, CSS for styling, and JavaScript for interactivity and behavior.
